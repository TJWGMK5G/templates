Frequently asked questions
  • Question: What is the difference between CPL and PPL? Answer: A Commercial Pilot License (CPL) allows you to get paid for flying, while a Private Pilot License (PPL) is for personal flying only. CPL training is more extensive and includes advanced flight maneuvers and commercial regulations.
  • Question: How long does it take to complete the CPL course? Answer: The duration of the CPL course can vary, but typically it takes around 6 to 12 months to complete, depending on the intensity of the program and the student's availability.
  • Question: What are the entry requirements for the CPL course? Answer: To enroll in a CPL course, you generally need to have a PPL, meet medical fitness standards, and pass a written entrance exam. Some institutes may also have age and educational prerequisites.
  • Question: Are there any financing options available for the training? Answer: Many aviation schools offer financing plans or partnerships with financial institutions to help students manage the cost of training. It's best to inquire directly with the institute for specific options.
  • Question: What kind of job placements or career support do you offer? Answer: We provide career support including job placement assistance, resume building, and interview preparation. Our network within the aviation industry helps connect graduates with potential employers.
  • Question: Can I start the training if I have no prior flying experience? Answer: Yes, you can start your training without any prior flying experience. Our programs are designed to take you from the basics all the way to obtaining your CPL or PPL.
Pilot Training in India
India has seen significant growth in the number of flying schools offering pilot training, providing exciting career opportunities in aviation. The Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) oversees civil and commercial aviation in the country, issuing pilot licenses across various levels. The Commercial Pilot License (CPL) is the fundamental qualification for those aiming to fly commercial aircraft.
