Why choose a Career as a Pilot?

A career as a pilot blends the excitement of flying with substantial professional and financial benefits. Pilots experience a dynamic work environment, operating large aircraft and flying international routes. The financial prospects are strong, with competitive salaries that grow with experience. This profession provides a unique aerial view of the world and offers job security due to the increasing demand for skilled pilots, making it both rewarding and financially rewarding.

Perks of being a pilot

Adventure and Exploration

Enjoy the thrill of traveling to new destinations and experiencing different cultures.

Dynamic Work Environment

Each flight brings new challenges and experiences, keeping your job exciting and varied.

Attractive Compensation

Benefit from competitive salaries and attractive financial packages that increase with experience.

Job Security

Enjoy strong job stability due to the ongoing global demand for qualified pilots.

Career Advancement

Opportunities for career growth and specialization in various types of aircraft and flying roles.

Your Journey

Become a Pilot at CAA

The sky isn’t the limit; it’s just the start at Chimes Aviation Academy!

1. Check Your Eligibility

Before taking flight, ensure you meet the prerequisites: educational background, age criteria, and medical fitness standards set by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) in India.

2. Choose Your License & Program

Decide whether you want a Private Pilot License (PPL) or a Commercial Pilot License (CPL). Select a program that aligns with your goals. CAA offers various ab initio programs for both PPL and CPL.

3. Obtain Your DGCA Computer Number

The Computer Number is essential for registering for DGCA exams and correspondence with the DGCA. Apply early, as the process can take over a month. Ensure you have the necessary documentation or a valid Computer Number when applying to CAA.

4. Complete the CAA Application Form

Submit your application form through CAA’s website. Keep an eye on updates via Social Media, the Website, and email newsletters. The form will require details such as educational qualifications, Medical Examination certificates, and other important documents.

5. Navigate the CAA Application Process

Once your application is reviewed, you may be invited to Ground School in Gurgaon, Haryana, for the ADAPT test. The final stages include an interview with the CAA admission panel and Group Discussions. Upon successful completion, you’ll receive your reporting date for Ground School induction.

6. Attend Ground School

Build a solid foundation with theoretical training and prepare for DGCA exams. This phase covers Air Regulations, Navigation, and more. At CAA, this training typically spans 6-8 months.

7. Engage in Hands-on Flying Training at CAA

Gain practical experience in the cockpit under the guidance of expert instructors. This hands-on training ensures you’re well-prepared for real-world flying scenarios, going beyond textbook knowledge.

8. Accumulate Quality Flight Hours

Focus on the quality of each flight hour. For CPL training, expect a 12-month cycle, providing exposure to diverse weather conditions across India. This comprehensive training ensures you’re adept at navigating various meteorological conditions.

9. Complete Check Rides and Practical Tests

Demonstrate your proficiency through check rides and practical tests conducted by certified examiners. These assessments validate your theoretical knowledge and flying skills, ensuring you meet DGCA’s standards.

10. Obtain Your Pilot License

After fulfilling all training requirements and DGCA standards, you’ll receive your pilot license. This credential signifies your skill and the high-quality training received at Chimes Aviation Academy.
